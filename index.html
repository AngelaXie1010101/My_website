About

A student builder with technical curiosity and visual taste.

I am a computer science student who enjoys building projects that combine problem solving, structure, and creativity. What draws me to computer science is the way it turns ideas into something real: a tool, a website, an experience, or a system that someone can actually use. I like the challenge of taking a concept, breaking it into smaller parts, and building it step by step into something functional.

At the same time, I am also interested in design because I care about presentation, clarity, and how users interact with what I build. I do not just want projects to work. I want them to feel organized, approachable, and intentional. That combination of technical thinking and visual design is what makes front-end work especially exciting to me.

So far, I have built multiple small web projects that have helped me grow as both a student and a developer. These include portfolio-style websites, a dictionary app, and other interactive projects that gave me practice with layout, styling, structure, and presenting information clearly. Each project has helped me improve not only my coding skills, but also my ability to think about the user experience and the purpose behind what I am making.

I am still learning, but that is a big part of what motivates me. I enjoy exploring new ideas, improving with each project, and building a body of work that reflects both curiosity and effort. Through future internships, research, and professional opportunities, I hope to keep developing projects that are thoughtful, useful, and well designed.
